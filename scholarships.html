Scholarships for students in food processing industries agriculture:


1)NIFTEM Merit Scholarship:

The scholarship will be available to first 3 students on merit in B.Tech. and one student on merit in each of the programme of M.Tech. Merit Scholarship would be paid during the period of second semester. There after toppers of first year will be awarded scholarship for next year and subsequent years accordingly only to the toppers of each year. It includes tuition fee waiver and a fixed monthly stipend for ten months.

source: visit site

2)NIFTEM Merit-cum-Means Scholarship:

Institute merit-cum-means scholarship is awarded to M.Tech. students of the institute strictly on merit-cum- means basis for needy students having family annual income from all sources not exceeding Rs. 2,50,000/-. 5% students will be considered for award of scholarship i.e. six students in M.Tech. (one each in all the five streams). It includes tuition fee waiver and a fixed monthly stipend for ten months.

source: visit site

4) DeHaven Scholarships:

The DeHaven Scholarships are specifically set aside for students who are pursuing a degree that will prepare them for a food service career. The annual award of $5,000 is given to those students who have shown a commitment to food service through employment, academics and extracurricular activities. Students must maintain a 2.75 GPA to qualify for the award.

source: visit site

5)Late Sri. A. Gopal Endowment Scholarship:

The University of Agricultural Sciences (UAS) in Bangalore provides this scholarship for B.Sc agriculture students in Karnataka. The students should have studied class I to class X in a rural area, and the scholarship is for one year.

Eligibility Criteria The eligibility criteria are:

The family income should not exceed INR 1,00,000 per annum. Students should hail from rural farming communities. They should have studied class I to class X in a rural area. Scholarship Benefits Each month, the selected candidate will receive a concession amount based on the interest accrued on the scholarship's total sum.

source: visit site

6)T.K. Gurusiddapa Scholarship:

The University of Agricultural Sciences (UAS) provides Smt. Swarnamba. T.K. Gurusiddapa scholarship. The scholarship applies to the students from Karnataka who want to pursue B.Sc in Agriculture. The scholarship is provided for twelve months.

Eligibility Criteria The eligibility criteria is as follows:

Candidates should be studying in the first year of B.Sc (Agri.). Family income should not exceed INR 1,00,000 per annum. Scholarship Benefits The UAS will give a concession amount. The amount is the sum of interest accrued on the scholarship each month.

source: visit site

7)R.N. Lakshmipathi and Smt. Nagarathnamma Merit Scholarship:

The University of Agricultural Sciences (UAS) provides Shri. R.N. Lakshmipathi and Smt. Nagarathnamma merit scholarship. It is exclusively for the female students of rural Karnataka who want to enrol in the B.Sc (Agri.) and B.Sc (Agri. Biotechnology) programs.

Eligibility Criteria The candidates should meet the following criteria:

The scholarship applies only to female students from the Chickballapur district. They should be pursuing B.Sc (Agri.) and B.Sc (Agri. Biotechnology). Candidates should have studied in the Kannada medium and should hail from rural Karnataka. They should have secured a minimum score of 7.5 CGPA in the first year. Family income should not exceed INR 1,00,000 per annum. Scholarship Benefits The students will receive the following benefits: The selected students will receive INR 875 per month. Interest accrued on the sum of INR 1,25,000 will equally be divided for twelve months

source: visit site

8)UAS Bangalore Sharans Vedanth Scholarship:

The University of Agricultural Science in Bangalore provides some of the best scholarships for agriculture students, including the Sharans Vedanth Scholarship. It applies to the rural students who want to pursue a B.Sc (Agri.) at the University of Agricultural Sciences, Bangalore.

Eligibility Criteria The eligibility criteria are as follows:

The candidates should apply for 1st year B.Sc (Agri.). They must be from the Kannada medium. The family should be working as farmers. The candidates should have studied class I to class X in the rural areas. Family income should not exceed INR 1,00,000 per annum. Scholarship Benefits A scholarship amount of INR 1,60,000 will be divided over twelve months.

source: visit site

9)Netaji Subhas-ICAR International Fellowships:

The ICAR (Indian Council of Agricultural Research) provides the Netaji Subhas-ICAR international fellowships for Indian students to study overseas. The fellowship program can also be applied by international students who wish to pursue an agricultural course in India. The duration of the program is for three years. The candidates must hold a postgraduate degree in agriculture and allied sciences and have secured an overall GPA score of 65%.

Eligibility Criteria The (Indian and international) applicants should meet the following criteria:

Should hold an M.A. degree in agriculture and allied sciences. Should have an overall GPA score of 65%. Should not be more than 35 years of age at the time of application. However, candidates serving at the ICAR-AU system are eligible to apply until 40 years of age. Scholarship Benefits The scholarship benefits are as follows: For Indian Students Studying Overseas ICAR will cover the economy flight tickets of Indian students travelling from their nearest airport to the place of their residence or university. The ICAR will give a scholarship amount of USD 2,000 every month. The scholarship also offers USD 1,000 per annum as contingent expenses to the selected candidates. For International Students Studying in India The institution will cover economy flight tickets of the international students. The international students will receive INR 40,000 per month. They will also receive INR 25,000 per annum as contingent expenses.

source: visit site

10)SAARC Agricultural PhD Scholarships:

SAARC Agriculture Centre (SAC) provides the SAARC agriculture PhD scholarship for three years. It is highly competitive, and the applicants will be selected based on merit. The scholarship will prioritise candidates with work experience in agriculture, biotechnology, and other related fields. The scholarship program is conducted in different SAARC member countries every year (except Afghanistan and Bangladesh).

Eligibility Criteria Mentioned below are the eligibility criteria:

The candidate should be employed at the National Agricultural Research, education and extension system in any SAARC member countries (except Afghanistan and Bangladesh). They should not be over 40 years of age at the time of application. They should have an excellent academic record. The applicants should be fluent in written and spoken English. Should hold a postgraduate degree in agricultural sciences, plant breeding, agricultural biotechnology, or cytogenetics from a recognised university. Scholarship Benefits The selected candidates will receive the following benefits: The organisation will cover the tuition fee and flight charges. SAC will offer a scholarship grant of USD 1,000. SAC will contribute USD 1,000 per annum towards research costs. The students will receive USD 600 per month as a living allowance for three years

source: visit site

11)The India Agri Fellowship:

The University of Commons provides the India Agri fellowship. Students aspiring to pursue an undergraduate or a postgraduate course can take up a year-long fellowship in Andhra Pradesh. The fellowship program's objective is to develop new farming ecosystems and increase farmers' growth in rural India. Each fellow will work with a group of farmers for the duration of the program.

Eligibility Criteria The candidates should fulfil the following criteria:

They should have an undergraduate or a postgraduate degree in agriculture, social work, or business administration. They should be in between 20 to 35 years of age. The candidates should be fluent in Telugu. Besides Telugu, they should know either English or Hindi language. They should be willing to work with the farmers in Andhra Pradesh. Scholarship Benefits The scholarship reward covers the living, travelling, and food expenses for 12 months. The selected candidates will receive INR 3,00,000 as a stipend amount for one year.

source: visit site

12)Profugo Field Fellowship:

Profugo is a non-profit organisation, and it provides the Profugo field fellowship. It is a highly competitive program, and young professionals or postgraduate students can apply for the fellowship. The field fellows get to coordinate a project at the Indian Center for Development and Rights in India. The duration of the program is 6 to 12 months.

Eligibility Criteria To be eligible, the candidates should meet the following requirements:

They should have good entrepreneurial skills. They should have a background in education, engineering, business, and agriculture. Candidates with cross-cultural experience will be preferred. Scholarship Benefits The selected candidates will receive the following: The organisation will cover the fellow's accommodation. They will also cover travel costs to the field placement. In addition, the organisation will offer stipends to the selected fellows
